## About

STIS Lab conducts research at the intersection of **cybersecurity**, **artificial intelligence**, **distributed learning**, **systems**, and **applied cryptography**. We develop algorithms, protocols, and system architectures that make intelligent systems **secure**, **dependable**, **verifiable**, and **deployable in practice**.

Our goal is to build next-generation intelligent systems that remain trustworthy in **adversarial**, **data-intensive**, and **resource-constrained** environments.

---

## Research Areas

### Cyber-Physical and Autonomous System Security
We study security and resilience for cyber-physical and autonomous systems, including industrial control systems, substations, autonomous platforms, and mission-critical infrastructures.

### Trustworthy Distributed Intelligence
We develop robust and adaptive distributed learning methods, including federated learning, asynchronous federated learning, and continual learning under non-IID data, partial participation, and adversarial settings.

### Post-Quantum and Verifiable Security
We investigate lightweight post-quantum cryptography, crypto-agility, secure AI model deployment, and verifiable security mechanisms for intelligent and resource-constrained systems.

### Privacy-Preserving AI and Data Systems
We study how data can be safely collected, shared, and utilized for AI without unnecessarily exposing sensitive information, enabling privacy-aware intelligence in modern data-driven systems.

### Secure LLM Systems and Human-AI Collaboration
We explore secure RAG systems, trustworthy AI agents, vulnerability detection in LLM-generated code, and human-AI collaboration for high-stakes and security-sensitive environments.
